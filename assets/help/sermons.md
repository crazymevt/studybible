# Sermons

The Sermons tool is a full writing studio for structured sermon notes and outlines—great for preparing to preach or for taking notes during a service.

> ✍️ **Not just for pastors:** Don't let the name put you off. The rich text editor is just as well suited to personal, in-depth Bible study—use it to work through a topic, gather the relevant passages in one place, and write out your own thoughts and reflections as you go.

## Creating a Sermon
1. Open the Sermons panel from the Study Tools menu, or the Sermons section from the App Drawer.
2. Tap the `+` button to create a new sermon.
3. Give it a title (e.g., "Sunday Morning: The Beatitudes") and an optional series name.
4. Write your notes using the rich text editor—headings, bold, italics, lists, and more.

## Outline Generator
Tap **Outline** to scaffold a sermon instantly. Choose how many points you want, and the app drops in an Introduction, numbered points (each with a Reference and Application line), and a Conclusion for you to fill in.

## Presentation Mode
Tap **Presentation Mode** for a clean, enlarged, distraction-free full-screen view of your notes—ideal for preaching or teaching from a tablet or laptop without squinting at small text.

## Navigate Scriptures
Tap **Navigate Scriptures** (the route icon) from the sermon editor or Presentation Mode to walk through every passage your sermon cites, in the order you wrote them.

- **Automatic route:** The app gathers each Bible reference in the sermon—single verses, ranges, and whole chapters alike—into an ordered list of stops. Repeats of the same passage back-to-back are merged, but a deliberate return to an earlier passage later on keeps its own stop.
- **Stepping through:** A slim bar appears under the Reader's breadcrumb with previous/next arrows and a position counter (e.g. `3/8`). It jumps the Reader straight to each passage as you step.
- **Jump anywhere:** Tap the current reference in the bar to open the full list of stops and jump to any one of them without stepping through the rest.
- **Temporary highlight:** Each stop's verses are highlighted for the session in all three reading layouts (flowing, parallel, and verse-by-verse). The highlight disappears when you end navigation—it never touches your saved highlights.
- **Ending:** Tap the `✕` on the bar to leave navigation mode.

> 💡 **Tip:** On narrow phone layouts the header buttons collapse into an overflow menu—find **Navigate Scriptures** there.

## Organizing & Finding
- **Series:** Group related sermons under a series name.
- **Tags:** Tap **Manage Tags** to file a sermon under tags like `#advent` or `#romans`. The same tags work across the whole app.
- **Search:** Sermons are included in the global search, so you can find any sermon by its words right alongside Bible and journal results.

### Searching, Filtering & Sorting the List
The Sermons panel has its own controls for working through a large collection:
- **Search box:** Type to filter the list by title, series name, or tag.
- **Tag chips:** Each sermon's colored tags show beside its series. Tap a chip to pin that tag and see only the sermons filed under it; tap again to clear.
- **Sort menu:** Reorder the list by title (A–Z or Z–A) or by date (newest or oldest first).
- **Pin to top:** Tap the pin icon on a sermon to keep it at the top of the list no matter how the list is sorted—handy for the sermon you're actively working on. Tap it again to unpin. Pins sync across your devices.

## Revision History
Every sermon keeps a history of past versions. Tap **Revision History** to look back at—and restore—an earlier draft, so you never lose work.

## Exporting & Printing
- **Export** a single sermon or all of them at once as:
  - **PDF** — a clean plain-text layout.
  - **HTML** — retains all of your rich formatting.
  - **Plain Text** — raw, unformatted text.
- **Print** sends the sermon straight to a connected printer.

> 💡 **Tip:** To quote Scripture in a sermon, copy a verse from the Reader (select it and tap **Copy**) and paste it into your notes. See **Notes, Highlights & Sharing**.

> 🔗 **Auto-linking:** Type a Bible reference with its verse—like `John 3:16` or `Rom 8:28-30`—and it turns into a link automatically. Tap the link to jump straight to that passage in the Reader—including from **Presentation Mode**. Capitalize the book name (`John`, not `john`) so it's recognized.
